# BeatsPerfect

BeatsPerfect is a bootstrap AI-operated digital product company.

Core principle:

> Good enough beats perfect.

More precisely:

> Build good-enough products inside explicit constraints.

Product means the buyer journey split into three first-class artifacts:

listing =
- title
- thumbnail
- image sequence
- promise
- trust signals
- objection handlers
- buyer-visible contents
- compatibility claims
- bonus visibility
- description
- pricing position

product =
- workbook
- tabs
- workflows
- formulas
- dashboards
- editable areas
- sample data
- outputs

delivery =
- quick start
- setup guide
- faq
- examples
- troubleshooting
- compatibility help

FLOW-006 keeps the three-part offer structure intact while preserving mandatory competitor inspection, founder acceptance, a machine-checkable JTBD/KPP/RTB product contract before build, and pre-launch failure analysis before delivery launch.
Market evidence determines what to offer.
Domain expertise determines how to implement and test the evidence-backed offer correctly.
Generation cost is budgeted separately from competitor purchase spend, and canonical model IDs are preferred over legacy aliases.

## Company Memory

Company-level rule changes must preserve their reasons in `docs/COMPANY-MEMORY.md`.

That memory is not a technical changelog. It records why flows, schemas, gates, budgets, validators, and governance rules changed, what failure caused the change, and what future failure the company is trying to prevent.

Material governance or flow changes are incomplete until the company memory is updated.

Good enough means:

- listing meets benchmark bestseller expectations
- product delivers the listing promise
- delivery enables first successful use
- addresses major recurring actionable complaints
- use human-friendly controls when promised, for example visible checkbox controls instead of exposed TRUE/FALSE spreadsheet values
- keep every listing claim verifiable against the delivered product
- run an evidence-bound pre-mortem on the complete product/listing/delivery offer before delivery launch
- stop building once the promise is met and recurring actionable complaints are addressed
- no critical defects
- launched within constraints
- capable of generating real outcome data
- product decisions stay anchored to observed market evidence, not copied competitor assets

Good Enough build philosophy:

listing:
- meets benchmark bestseller expectations

product:
- delivers the listing promise

improvements:
- addresses major recurring actionable complaints

forbidden:
- add unsupported claims
- add unsupported features

Perfect is not the target.

Launchable is the target.

## Objective

Reach and maintain at least $10,000/month net profit.

Milestones:

- Month 3 after launch: at least $3,000/month net profit
- Month 6 after launch: at least $10,000/month net profit
- After reaching $10,000/month: never below $10,000 for more than one consecutive month

## Operating Model

The company does not forecast revenue creatively.
The company tracks model token usage as first-class operating data and derives API cost from that usage when possible.

The company uses evidence-based admission:

Idea
→ Tier 1 structural screen
→ BM-001 market viability screen
→ Candidate
→ FIFO build queue
→ Build lifecycle
→ Marketplace publish
→ Product V1.0
→ Monthly outcomes
→ Kill / bug fix / double down

## Human-Owned Decisions

Humans own:

- company objective
- constraints
- purchases
- advertising spend
- governance changes
- admission-rule changes
- kill-rule changes
- resource-allocation-rule changes
- marketplace publish approval when required

Agents may execute inside the rules.

Agents may not change the rules.

## Agent Rule

Agents must not invent evidence, market facts, costs, outcomes, or product scope.

If evidence is missing, reject or escalate.

If a rule is unclear, escalate rather than improvise.

## Product Versioning

- V1.0 = first launchable version
- V1.x = bug fix
- V2.0 = first double down
- V3.0 = second double down
- product_id never changes

## Learning Philosophy

Outcome first.

Finding later.

Company-level findings are postponed until enough real product data exists, initially at least 20 launched products.
